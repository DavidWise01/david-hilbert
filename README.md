# DAVID HILBERT · XVII

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **LOGIKĒ**.

DAVID HILBERT (1862–1943) — LOGIKĒ XVII, the FINAL BOSS of this domain: nearly every result here was an answer to a question HE posed. The towering mathematician of his age set the 20th century's agenda with the 23 PROBLEMS (1900) and dreamed the boldest dream in logic — ⚑ HILBERT'S PROGRAM: prove all mathematics COMPLETE (every truth provable) + CONSISTENT (no contradictions) + DECIDABLE (a mechanical procedure settles any statement), by safe finitary reasoning. ⚑ THE ANSWER it received: [[kurt-godel]] 1931 killed completeness & self-proved consistency; [[alonzo-church]] & [[alan-turing]] 1936 killed decidability (the Entscheidungsproblem) — and to prove 'no machine decides all math' they had to DEFINE what a machine is = the computer. His program failed more productively than almost anything has succeeded; the failure IS the foundation of [[psephos-processors-domain]]. LIVE: HILBERT'S HOTEL — a hotel with infinitely many rooms, all full, that still takes more guests: one new guest → everyone shifts n→n+1, room 0 opens (nobody evicted); an infinite bus → everyone moves n→2n, every ODD room opens (verified mappings) — Cantor's infinity made playable. CONFIDENCE-TIERED: STRONG (the axiomatic method, the 23 problems, Grundlagen der Geometrie fixing Euclid's gaps, Hilbert spaces in quantum mechanics — monumental, shaped all modern math & physics) / THE-PROGRAM-FAILED (⚠ his SPECIFIC dream was proven impossible — math can't be complete+consistent+decidable — but the failure BIRTHED computability theory) / 'WE-WILL-KNOW'-NOT-ALL (his epitaph 'Wir müssen wissen, wir werden wissen' promises no unknowables; there ARE true-but-unprovable & undecidable statements — the quest for total knowledge was unreachable and built CS on the way). The man: led Göttingen, the world's math center, and watched the Nazis DESTROY it after 1933 (purging his Jewish colleagues — asked if math at Göttingen suffered from 'the departure of the Jews': 'Suffered? It doesn't exist any longer'); died 1943 in the emptied city. [[alonzo-church]] ← · next → Łukasiewicz.
